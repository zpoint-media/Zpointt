A Landmark Year: Drama, Records, and Rising Stars in the 2026 Golf Major Championships

21 July 2026

The 2026 Golf Major Championships delivered one of the most thrilling seasons in modern golf history. From emotional victories to dramatic collapses, and from rising stars to legendary comebacks, each major served unforgettable moments that will be remembered for decades.

With four different champions across the Masters, PGA Championship, U.S. Open, and The Open, the season showcased the depth of global talent and the evolving landscape of men’s professional golf.

Scheffler dominates Augusta National for second Masters title

Scottie Scheffler continued to solidify his status as the world’s best golfer, capturing his second green jacket with a commanding performance at Augusta National. His final-round 67 kept him ahead of a charging field, including Viktor Hovland and Collin Morikawa.

“This win means more than words,” Scheffler said. “Augusta is sacred ground, and every moment out here feels larger than life.”

The year’s second major, the PGA Championship at Valhalla, delivered the season’s biggest shock when 23-year-old Japanese sensation Riku Matsuyama claimed his first career major. With fearless iron play and unmatched composure under pressure, he edged out Xander Schauffele by one shot.

“I didn’t expect this moment to come so soon,” said Matsuyama. “But I’ve worked for it all my life.”

The U.S. Open at Shinnecock Hills tested the world’s best with brutal winds and lightning-fast greens. Rory McIlroy led after 54 holes but faltered in the final round, allowing American Patrick Cantlay to claim his first U.S. Open trophy with a steady, tactical approach.

The season concluded in style at Royal Birkdale, where The Open Championship delivered pure links golf drama. In a tense three-way playoff, England’s Tommy Fleetwood overcame Jordan Spieth and Ludvig Åberg to celebrate an emotional home-soil victory.

“To lift the Claret Jug in front of my home fans… I can’t put it into words,” Fleetwood said tearfully.

A season that reshaped the golf landscape

With four champions from four different countries, the 2026 majors highlighted the global reach and competitive balance in today’s game. Analysts say the sport is entering a golden era — defined by deeper fields, stronger young talent, and more unpredictable outcomes.

As the world looks ahead to the 2027 season, questions remain: Can Scheffler maintain dominance? Will McIlroy finally secure his long-awaited next major? And are we witnessing the rise of a new generational star in Riku Matsuyama?
