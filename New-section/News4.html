Kraft Shines as Germany Hosts Thrilling Viessmann FIS Ski Jumping World Cup

18 Jan 2026

The Viessmann FIS Ski Jumping World Cup returned to Germany with electric atmosphere as thousands of fans filled the hillsides for another unforgettable weekend of world-class jumping. Austria’s Stefan Kraft delivered a remarkable performance, securing victory in Saturday’s individual event and extending his lead in the overall World Cup standings.

Germany’s own Karl Geiger impressed the home crowd with exceptional form, finishing second after a powerful final jump that sent the arena into a frenzy.

Kraft dominates with flawless technique

Kraft showed why he remains one of the most consistent jumpers of his generation. His first jump set the tone early, flying past the 140m mark with stunning stability and landing control. He described it as “one of the best flights of the season,” crediting the German crowd for the energy.

“I felt amazing in the air today,” Kraft said. “The conditions were fair, the hill was perfect, and the support from fans pushed all of us to our limits.”

German spectators were especially vocal, waving flags and chanting throughout the event, creating one of the best atmospheres of the season.

In Sunday’s competition, Norway’s Marius Lindvik responded strongly, scoring the day’s best jump and claiming the top podium spot. Kraft settled for second place, maintaining his overall advantage.

Germany once again celebrated a podium moment as Andreas Wellinger took third, showing consistency on home soil and keeping himself in the fight for season points.

“The energy in Germany is unmatched,” Wellinger said. “This hill brings out the best in me every year.”

The battle for the Crystal Globe continues to intensify, with Kraft, Lindvik, and Geiger emerging as the top contenders heading into the remaining events.

The German team expressed satisfaction with their performance, especially with strong jumps from younger athletes gaining valuable experience.

“We are building something special,” said Germany’s coach. “Week after week, the team grows more confident. Competing at home is always an emotional boost.”

With the world’s attention now turning to the next stop on the World Cup tour, Germany’s weekend delivered spectacle, drama, and exceptional athletic skill — a true highlight of the 2025/2026 season.

A successful and unforgettable weekend in Germany

The Viessmann FIS Ski Jumping World Cup in Germany once again proved why it remains one of the most iconic and passionate venues on the circuit. From record-setting jumps to roaring fans, the event showcased the very heart of ski jumping.
